# Course 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity

---

## 📘 Module 1: Introduction to Cybersecurity

### Key Concepts
- Cybersecurity focuses on protecting systems, networks, and data from threats.
- Security analysts monitor systems, detect breaches, and prevent incidents.
- Security posture defines how well an organization protects its assets.

---

### Important Terms

- **CIA Triad**: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability model  
- **Compliance**: Following laws and regulations  
- **Security Frameworks**: Structured guidelines for managing security risks  
- **Security Controls**: Safeguards used to reduce risks  
- **Threat Actor**: Person or group causing harm  
- **Internal Threat**: Insider risk (intentional or accidental)  
- **Cloud Security**: Protecting data and systems in cloud environments  
- **Network Security**: Protecting network infrastructure  

---

## 📘 Module 2: Evolution of Cybersecurity

### Key Concepts
- Cyber threats evolved from early viruses like Morris Worm to modern attacks.
- Organizations use incident response teams (CSIRTs) to manage attacks.

---

### Phishing Attacks
- **Phishing**: Trick users into revealing sensitive data  
- **BEC**: Fake business email for financial gain  
- **Spear Phishing**: Targeted attack  
- **Whaling**: Targets executives  
- **Vishing**: Voice-based attacks  
- **Smishing**: SMS-based attacks  

---

### Malware Types
- **Virus**: Requires human interaction  
- **Worm**: Self-replicating  
- **Ransomware**: Locks data for money  
- **Spyware**: Collects user data secretly  

---

### Social Engineering
- Exploits human trust and behavior  

#### Key Techniques:
- Authority
- Urgency
- Fear / Intimidation
- Trust & Familiarity
- Scarcity

---

## 📘 Module 4: Cybersecurity Tools & Technologies

### Tools and Concepts
- **SIEM**: Monitors logs and security events  
- **IDS**: Detects intrusions  
- **Antivirus**: Prevents malware  
- **Logs**: Record system activity  
- **Packet Sniffer**: Captures network traffic

## 🎯 My Key Learning

- Cybersecurity involves people, processes, and technology.  
- Most attacks exploit human behavior (social engineering).  
- Understanding threats is as important as using tools.  
- Security frameworks help organizations stay organized and protected.
